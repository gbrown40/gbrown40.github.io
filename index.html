Palmer Penguins Traits

Grace Brown

Motivation

In this assignment, I looked at the traits of three different penguin species (Adelie, Chinstrap, and Gentoo). Studying the physical characteristics of penguins and the differences across species is very fascinating, and learning more about the natural world around is very important.

Data

This dataset is obtained from https://allisonhorst.github.io/palmerpenguins/, made publically available by Dr. Kristen Gorman and the Palmer Station. I chose this dataset as it is interesting to see and study the physical traits of animals. Looking at the data, three different penguin species are examined, each from one of three different islands. The data is also labeled by sex. After some initial data exploration, male penguins have higher values on average across the penguins' physical traits.

Data Visualization(s)

Body Mass vs Bill Depth by Species

In this scatter plot, body mass is plotted against bill depth, colored by the species type. Color was used here to easily identify which data points belong to which species and to see if species clusters form, which they do. I kept the default color scheme as it distinguished between the species types well, especially for Adelie and Chinstrap which have a great deal of overlap. I chose a scatter plot to be able to see the general correlation between body mass and bill depth while still being able to look at each individual data point. When looking at each species cluster, bill depth increases as body mass increases. When looking at the full chart, though, this is not the case. As seen above, the Gentoo species, while higher on average in body mass than the other two species, has lower on average bill depth. This chart is also interactive so that users can obtain more information as desired. Hovering over data points brings up their specific values. This makes it easy to consider the values of individual points without cluttering the chart. Hovering over data points also greys out the rest of the data, making it so the user can consider a specific point without being distracted by the rest of the data. The chart also supports scrolling to change the scale. This allows users to zoom in and out to look at specific regions of the data or consider the plot as a whole.

Distribution of Flipper Length across Penguin Species

In this violin chart, the distribution of flipper length by species is plotted, colored by the species type. Color was used here to easily distinguish species type. I chose the viridis color scheme as it distinguishes between the species well but gives a natural feel to the chart with its green and blue tones, which seemed appropriate for examining wild species. I used a violin chart to be able to see the full distribution of the data. As shown above, the Adelie and Chinstrap species have very similar distributions. The Gentoo species has a more bottom heavy distribution, however. This chart is also interactive so that users can obtain more information as desired. The chart supports scrolling to change the scale. This allows users to zoom in and out to look more closely at the shapes of the distributions. .

The topic of my infographic is the physical characteristics of penguin species. This topic is interesting as it shows the differences in physical traits between penguin species, allowing us to better understand penguins and thus the natural world around us. I want the viewer of this infographic to gain a better understanding of how penguins vary by species and to become more curious about penguins on the whole.
